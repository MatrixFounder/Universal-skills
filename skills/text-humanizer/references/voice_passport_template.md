# Voice Passport Template

When the user provides writing samples, analyze them along these five dimensions to create a "voice passport." Use this passport as a reference during rewriting to maintain consistency with the author's natural style.

## Dimensions

### 1. Rhythm
*   **Average sentence length:** Short (5-10 words), Medium (10-20), Long (20+), or Mixed?
*   **Variability:** Does the author alternate between short punchy and long winding sentences, or stay consistent?
*   **Favorite constructions:** Fragments? Questions? Lists? Parenthetical asides? Compound sentences chained with commas?

### 2. Lexicon
*   **Formality level:** 1 (texting a friend) to 10 (academic paper)
*   **Jargon:** Does the author use domain-specific terms without explaining them?
*   **Register mixing:** Does formal prose occasionally drop into slang or vice versa?
*   **Colloquialisms:** Contractions? Informal phrases? Regional expressions?

### 3. Quirks
*   **Signature phrases:** Recurring expressions, pet words, habitual openers or closers.
*   **Digressions:** Does the author go on tangents? Use footnote-style asides?
*   **Humor style:** Dry? Sarcastic? Self-deprecating? Absent?

### 4. Punctuation
*   **Preferred marks:** Heavy on dashes? Ellipses? Parentheses? Semicolons? Exclamation points?
*   **Density:** Minimal punctuation (clean, flowing) or heavy punctuation (precise, segmented)?
*   **Unconventional usage:** Multiple question marks?? Em dashes for dramatic effect? Periods. For. Emphasis.

### 5. Tone
*   **Default stance:** Confident? Tentative? Provocative? Neutral? Warm?
*   **Emotional range:** Does the author express frustration, excitement, doubt openly?
*   **Relationship to reader:** Teaching? Confiding? Challenging? Entertaining?

## Output Format

Summarize the voice passport in 3-5 lines. Example:

> **Voice:** Short sentences dominate (8-12 words), with occasional long ones that pile up clauses. Formality 4/10. Heavy on contractions and "look," "here's the thing," "honestly." Dry humor, self-deprecating. Lots of dashes and parenthetical asides. Confident but admits uncertainty openly ("I might be wrong, but..."). Talks to the reader like a coworker, not an audience.

Refer back to this passport during every rewriting decision. When in doubt, ask: "Would this author write it this way?"
